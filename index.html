About Us

New Hope Community Bikes started in 2008 as an initiative of New Hope Church. We're a non-profit registered charity, and we seek to get more people on affordable, reliable bicycles and to provide employment and job training opportunities for youth.

We believe bicycles can help build a healthier, more environmentally friendly community; we believe that everyone should have access to affordable and efficient transportation. We build and restore bikes at a variety of price points and rider specifications, offering everything from cheap winter commuters to unique, one-of-a-kind custom bikes.

What we do

  • Accept donated bikes of all shapes and sizes
  • Refurbish bikes donated bicycles
  • Sell affordable bikes, parts, tools and accessories
  • Provide bicycle repair education through structured classes and weekly shop hours.
  • Provide repairs to all makes and models of bikes
  • Provide tools, workstations, and expertise for DIYers
  • Provide meaningful volunteer opportunities for youth and adults
  • Lead cycle safety education for school groups, community groups, and individuals

What we don't do

  • Purchase used bikes
  • Trade bikes
  • Lend tools off-site
  • Sell unsafe bikes
  • Work on suspension forks. Why? Suspension forks require many brand specific oils and fittings. We don't keep these in stock but we can recommend some good shops to have your fork looked at.

Repair classes

We offer structured bike repair classes on a monthly basis. These classes will teach you how to do your own repairs, and be more comfortable overall around a bike. For times and more details go here.

Designated shop nights

We offer women/trans/queer only events. For more information about what drives us to run these events, you can read this post, or see this article. WTQ basic bike repair workshops (a three-hour structured course) occur on the first Tuesday of each month and require registration in advance; the form can be found here. WTQ open shop nights occur on the third Tuesday of each month and require no advance registration. Just drop in and work on your bike with as much or as little help as you need!

The shop is used every Friday night by Restoration Hamilton for their community gathering. Every 3rd Friday of every month, Restoration Hamilton holds a church in the bike shop. Everyone is welcome—there's good food and good fellowship!

DIY (Do-It-Yourself)

New Hope Community Bikes is a non-profit and exists for anyone wishing to use our space. Tools and shop space is available whenever the shop is open on a first-come-first-served basis. This means if it's busy you may have to wait for someone to finish at a workspace before you can start your own DIY. We offer a sliding scale for tool use and stand space: $0-5/hour if you do the work yourself, and $0-10/hour if we help out. If you don't have the money to pay, that's okay. Pay what you can, or pay next time.
